BEER

A brief guide.

Types of beer

  • Lager - One of the two major beer types, lagers use bottom-fermenting yeast that requires colder temperatures to ferment and generally produces a "cleaner" tasting beer.
    • Pilsner - A light colored, hop-forward (for the time when it was invented) lager first breweed in Plizen in the Czech Republic.
    • Helles - The German's answer to the increasingly popular Chezch pils, Helles lager is maltier than the Czech version
  • Ale - The other, older major type of beer that uses a top-fermenting yeast that can ferment at higher temperatures and produces more complex and varied flavors than most lagers.
    • Pale Ale - A lightly colored, often hop-focused ale that is a staple of most American craft breweries
    • India Pale Ale or IPA - A hop-forward beer that supposedly recieved an extra dose of hops and a higher alcohol content so that it could survive the voyage from England to India.
